Protection

Cybersecurity is not just tools — it’s habits. Below are practical steps that reduce risk, protect your accounts, and help keep your data safe.

Review common threats →

Account & Identity Protection

These steps focus on preventing account takeovers and protecting login information.

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Adds an extra verification step to prevent unauthorized account access.

Strong Passwords

Using long, unique passwords reduces the risk of account compromise.

Recovery Email

Helps regain account access if credentials are stolen or lost.

Access Control (ACL)

Limits user access based on roles to minimize security risks.

Device, Data & Awareness

These steps reduce malware risk, protect important data, and help users avoid scams.

Regular Backups

Allows data recovery after ransomware or system failure.

Safe Downloads

Prevents malware by installing software only from trusted sources.

Antivirus Software

Detects and blocks malicious programs on devices.

Data Encryption

Protects sensitive data by converting it into unreadable form so only authorized users can access it, even if the data is stolen.

User Education

Teaching users how to recognize scams reduces human error.

Precaution Awareness

Verifying links and requests before acting helps prevent attacks.

Network Monitoring

Identifies suspicious activity early to reduce damage.

Quick Checklist

Small actions make a big difference. Check off what you can do today.
