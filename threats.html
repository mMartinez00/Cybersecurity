Cyber Threats

Cyber threats take many forms and often target everyday users through email, websites, and unsecured networks. Below are common cyber attacks explained in simple terms to help you recognize them and reduce risk.

Social & Human-Based Attacks

These threats rely on manipulation, urgency, or reused passwords to gain access.

Phishing

Fake emails, calls, or messages trick users into clicking links or sharing login details.

Learn more
  • Common signs: urgent tone, unfamiliar sender, suspicious links.
  • Example: “Your account will be locked—verify now.”

Credential Stuffing

Attackers use leaked passwords from other sites to take over accounts.

Learn more
  • Why it works: people reuse passwords across websites.
  • Where it happens: email, social media, shopping accounts.

Social Engineering

Manipulating people by impersonating trusted individuals to gain sensitive information.

Learn more
  • Common tactic: pretending to be IT support, a boss, or a bank.
  • Goal: get passwords, verification codes, or money.

Malware-Based Threats

These threats involve harmful software that damages devices or blocks access to files.

Malware

Harmful software that enters devices through fake updates, attachments, or unsafe downloads.

Learn more
  • Common sources: shady downloads, email attachments, pop-ups.
  • Impact: spyware, data theft, or system damage.

Ransomware

Malicious software encrypts files and demands payment to restore access.

Learn more
  • Impact: you can lose access to important files.
  • Best defense: backups + updates + safe downloads.

Network & Web Attacks

These threats target networks, websites, or data moving between systems.

Man-in-the-Middle (MITM)

Intercepting data between users and networks, often on public Wi-Fi.

Learn more
  • Risk: attackers may capture logins or personal info.
  • Common scenario: unsecured public Wi-Fi.

Web Application Attacks

Exploiting website vulnerabilities (such as SQL injection) to steal or damage data.

Learn more
  • Target: websites and web apps with weak security.
  • Impact: data leaks, defacement, service disruption.

DDoS Attacks

Flooding a website or server with traffic to make it unavailable.

Learn more
  • Impact: websites become slow or completely unavailable.
  • Target: businesses, schools, and online services.

Common Warning Signs

  • Messages that create urgency or pressure you to act quickly
  • Requests for passwords, verification codes, or payment
  • Unexpected links or attachments
  • Websites that look familiar but have unusual URLs
